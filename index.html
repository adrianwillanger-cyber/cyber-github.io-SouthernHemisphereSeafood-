Southern Hemisphere Seafood Market Report – May 2026

Export performance across Chile, Argentina, Australia, and New Zealand with emphasis on crab, salmon, and whitefish flows.

Market Overview

Southern Hemisphere producers entered Q2 with stable supply conditions and moderate pricing pressure. Chilean salmon continues to anchor regional export value, while Argentina’s crab and prawn shipments remain constrained by quota timing and vessel availability. Australia and New Zealand show steady whitefish and shellfish volumes, with Japan and China remaining the primary demand centers.

Export Flows

Chile drove most of the regional uplift, with salmon exports rising on improved harvest weights and favorable feed conversion. Argentina’s crab exports were flat due to vessel downtime and quota pacing. New Zealand’s hoki and snapper shipments increased modestly, supported by stable EU demand. Australia’s rock lobster exports remain below historical norms due to ongoing China market adjustments.

Pricing Trends

Pricing held within a narrow band across most species. Chilean salmon prices softened slightly as inventories normalized. Argentine crab pricing remained firm due to limited supply and strong Asian interest. Whitefish pricing across NZ and AU was stable, with minor upward pressure from freight costs.

Species Notes

• Salmon: Chile remains dominant; biomass conditions stable.
• Crab: Argentina’s supply tight; premium grades commanding higher bids.
• Whitefish: NZ hoki steady; AU snapper volumes consistent.
• Lobster: AU exports still adjusting to diversified market mix.

Risks & Opportunities

Risks include currency volatility (CLP and ARS), quota pacing in Argentina, and freight rate fluctuations. Opportunities lie in diversified Asian demand, particularly South Korea and Vietnam, and improving EU interest in NZ whitefish.

Summary

The Southern Hemisphere market remains stable with modest growth. Chile continues to anchor regional performance, while Argentina’s crab sector benefits from tight supply. Pricing is balanced, with no major shocks expected in the near term.
